§ 01 THE THESIS

Aluminum used to be more precious than gold.

Napoleon served honored guests on aluminum. Regular guests got gold. Then the Hall-Héroult process arrived, and aluminum became the cheap stuff we crumple and throw out.

Intelligence is on the same trajectory. The cognitive revolution is doing for thinking what the industrial revolution did for thrust, lift, and torque.

Most regulated manufacturers will get this wrong. Not because the technology is hard — because the vendors selling them AI have never released a Nadcap batch, never argued with a DER, never watched a heat-treat soak run three minutes long and rewrite next month's schedule.

We have. Cognitive Factory exists because the people who actually run the floor should own the AI deployed on it.
